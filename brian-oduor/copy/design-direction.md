# Design Direction - Brian Oduor

## Visual Goal
Create a premium, calm, and credibility-first personal portfolio that feels refined, modern, and human.

## Brand Feeling
- Confident
- Polished
- Warm
- Trustworthy
- Minimal without feeling cold

## Color Direction
- Deep burgundy: primary accent that complements the suit in the headshot
- Ink navy: for structure, depth, and headline contrast
- Warm ivory: for the main background and breathing room
- Soft gold: for subtle highlights and premium detail
- Cool slate: for supporting text and dividers

## Typography Mood
- Headings: an elegant serif with presence and character
- Body: a clean sans-serif with strong readability
- Type feel: editorial, composed, and modern
- Avoid generic corporate defaults and overly playful display fonts

## Layout Direction
- Hero-first composition with the supplied headshot as the visual anchor
- Large, confident name treatment
- Short, high-impact supporting copy
- Card-based sections with generous spacing
- Strong contrast between hero and supporting content
- Mobile layout should stack cleanly without losing hierarchy

## UI Style
- Soft gradient or textured background instead of flat white
- Rounded but restrained cards
- Subtle shadows and borders
- One strong accent color used sparingly
- Clear CTA buttons with distinct primary and secondary styling
- Simple navigation with anchor links

## Imagery Direction
- Use the provided portrait in the hero section
- Generate and link favicon from the same image
- Generate and link social card image at `assets/og-image.jpg`
- Keep image presentation crisp and intentional

## Interaction Direction
- Gentle reveal effects on scroll
- Light hover states on cards and buttons
- No heavy motion, no distracting effects
- Keep transitions smooth and understated

## Copy Treatment
- Use the supplied copy exactly as the source of truth
- Keep lines short and scannable
- Let the portrait and typography carry most of the visual weight

## Overall Direction
This should feel like a premium personal brand landing page rather than a generic resume site. The page should be sparse, elegant, and easy to trust at a glance.
