# Design Direction - Hilary Njiru Njoroge

## Brand Positioning
Professional, trustworthy, and structured personal brand for an administrative specialist. The website should communicate reliability, precision, and readiness to support modern office operations.

## Visual Tone
- Clean and polished
- Corporate but warm
- Detail-oriented and process-driven

## Color System
- Primary: `#0F3D5E` (Deep Blue - trust and professionalism)
- Secondary: `#1F6F8B` (Teal Blue - clarity and calm)
- Accent: `#2E8B57` (Structured Green - growth and efficiency)
- Surface: `#F6F9FC` (Soft Light Background)
- Text Primary: `#102A43`
- Text Secondary: `#486581`

## Typography Mood
- Headings: confident, contemporary, high legibility
- Body text: neutral sans-serif, comfortable spacing, business readable
- Tone cues: concise, clear, not verbose

## Layout and Component Guidance

### Global Layout
- Desktop-first responsive layout with clean mobile stacking.
- Use generous whitespace and clear section separation.
- Keep section ordering intuitive: Hero -> Proof -> About -> Experience -> Skills -> Contact.

### Header / Navigation
- Sticky top navigation with straightforward links.
- Include clear CTA button (`Book a Consultation`) in header.

### Hero Section
- Prominent name and role.
- Short, high-trust positioning statement.
- Use provided user image as hero visual.
- Place primary and secondary CTAs side by side on desktop, stacked on mobile.

### Proof and Metrics
- Use metric cards for key outcomes (30%, 25%, 20%, 98%, 70+).
- Keep copy factual and concise.

### Experience Section
- Timeline or card-based layout with role, organization, date range, and achievements.
- Highlight measurable outcomes in bold visual callouts.

### Skills Section
- Split into two groups: Professional Skills and Tools & Systems.
- Use icon-supported chips or compact cards for scanability.

### Contact Section
- Keep direct and action-oriented.
- Surface email, phone, location, and LinkedIn clearly.
- Include strong CTA and simple contact pathway.

## Accessibility and UX Rules
- Minimum AA contrast across text/background combinations.
- Semantic heading hierarchy (`h1` to `h3` as needed).
- Keyboard-focus visible on all interactive elements.
- Buttons and links must have clear hover and focus states.
- Ensure images include meaningful `alt` text.

## Motion and Interaction
- Subtle fade/slide-in on section reveal.
- Keep animations minimal and professional.
- Avoid distracting motion effects.

## Metadata and Media Policy
- Use generated `assets/favicon.png` as favicon.
- Use `assets/og-image.jpg` for Open Graph and Twitter large summary card metadata.
- Include required social metadata tags for image mode.

## Visual Reference Notes
- Portrait and profile image should feel natural and professional.
- Design language should support credibility for administrative and office support roles.
